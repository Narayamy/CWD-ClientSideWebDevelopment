Where do They live?

These animals can live in a variety of environments throughout north and South America. They are most abundant in the desert sands but also found in grasslands, scrub brush and rocky hills. They can handle high elevation like 11,000 feet from sea level.

What do They like?

Rattlesnakes like to spend time in dens made in rocky crevices where they also hibernate in winter when the climate is colder. As they are ectothermic animals, which mean they are not able to produce their own heat, they like to sun themselves on rocks and open places.

One of its predators is the king snake who is immune to rattlesnake’s venom and includes it on their menu.

Rattlesnakes are ovoviviparous which mean the eggs incubate inside the mother’s body. After fertilized the mother carries the babies for about three months. They only give birth every two years and the mother does not have any parental care with the babies.

Rattlesnakes can live for 10 to 25 years.
